Chemical Reaction Engineering

Batch Reactor Simulation (A → B → C)

Posted on June 25, 2025

Overview:

Reactors are the heart of any chemical process. In this project, I focused on modeling a Batch Reactor undergoing a series reaction mechanism: A → B → C. The goal was to determine the precise time required to maximize the concentration of the intermediate product (B) before it degrades into product C.

The Challenge

In series reactions, the intermediate product often has the highest value. If the reaction runs too long, Product B converts to C (waste). If it's too short, conversion of A is insufficient.

The Solution (MATLAB & Simulink)

I used MATLAB to solve the Ordinary Differential Equations (ODEs) governing the kinetics:

  • dCa/dt = -k1 * Ca
  • dCb/dt = k1 * Ca - k2 * Cb
  • dCc/dt = k2 * Cb

Using Simulink, I visualized the concentration profiles over time, allowing me to pinpoint the exact residence time needed for peak efficiency.
